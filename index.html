Quaid e Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

The Founder of Pakistan

Here's a time line Quaid e Azam's life:

  • 1876 - Born on December 25 in Karachi, then part of British India.
  • 1896 - Travels to England to study law at Lincoln's Inn.
  • 1913 - Joins the All-India Muslim League, advocating for Muslim rights.
  • 1920s - Becomes a prominent leader in the demand for Indian independence.
  • 1940 - Lahore Resolution calls for the establishment of a separate Muslim state.
  • 1947 - Pakistan is created on August 14; Jinnah becomes its first Governor-General.
  • 1948 - Jinnah dies on September 11 in Karachi.
"Jinnah was the architect of our nation, and his vision and leadership laid the foundation for Pakistan. His dedication and unwavering resolve inspired millions."
--Liaquat Ali Khan, The First Prime Minister of Pakistan
